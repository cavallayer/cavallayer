# Systems Circle — Charter

---

## Purpose

Systems Circle exists to provide a high-signal environment for people who build and govern high-stakes systems — and who carry moral seriousness about the consequences of that work.

The circle is not a network. It is not a support group. It is not a mastermind.
It is a structured, rigorous, recurring forum for people who think in systems and govern by plumb line.

---

## Who We Are

Members of Systems Circle:

- Build, govern, or operate systems where decisions have real consequences for real people
- Think in structure, incentives, constraints, failure modes, and governance
- Carry responsibility without theatrics
- Value moral grounding without performance
- Prefer directness over politics
- Operate from a Judeo-Christian moral framework or are in serious dialogue with one

---

## What We Commit To

As a member of Systems Circle, you commit to:

- Attending monthly sessions prepared to contribute structured thinking
- Bringing a real system challenge when it is your turn
- Engaging with others' challenges with rigor, not encouragement
- Holding what is shared in the circle in confidence
- Operating by your plumb line — and being honest when you have not
- Accepting challenge without defensiveness
- Delivering challenge without cruelty

---

## Structure

- **Format:** Global, virtual, monthly
- **Duration:** 55 minutes — ends on time, every time
- **Size:** Small cohorts, curated for signal density
- **Cohorts:** May expand into specialty groups — clinical, startup, engineering, by region — as membership grows
- **Platform:** Discord

---

## Membership

Membership is by application and invitation.
The founding members review all applications and issue invitations.
Membership continues at the discretion of the founding members.
There is no appeal process.
We curate for signal density, not size.

---

## Origin

Systems Circle was founded by Sarah Curtright, DNP, MBA — clinician, founder of Kairoc Systems, and author of the CAVAL open standard for clinical AI governance.

Systems Circle grew from a recognition that serious systems thinkers — especially those who carry moral grounding — rarely find peers who operate at that level without the noise of networking culture, encouragement culture, or devotional culture.

This circle is the alternative.
