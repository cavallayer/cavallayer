# Systems Circle — Membership Application

Minimal. Architectural. Signal over noise.

Read the [charter](charter.md) and [norms](norms.md) before applying.

**To apply:** Copy all questions below, write your answers, and email to [securtright@gmail.com](mailto:securtright@gmail.com) with subject line: **Systems Circle Application — [Your Name]**

There are no word limits. There is no right answer. There is no wrong format. Answer as you think, not as you think you should think.

---

## Section 0 — Contact

- Q1. Name
- Q2. Email
- Q3. LinkedIn (optional)
- Q4. Website / GitHub / Portfolio (optional)

---

## Section 1 — Identity

- Q5. What system do you build, govern, or operate?
- Q6. What is the core problem your system exists to solve?
- Q7. What is the highest-stakes decision your system makes or influences?
- Q8. What is the most important constraint in your system?
- Q9. What is the clearest signal that your system is failing?

---

## Section 2 — Architecture & Reasoning

- Q10. Describe a failure mode in your system and how you identified it.
- Q11. Describe a decision you made where clarity mattered more than consensus.
- Q12. Describe a time you imposed structure on ambiguity.

---

## Section 3 — Operating Style

- Q13. How do you prefer to receive challenge?
- Q14. What does accountability look like in your work?
- Q15. What do you do when something ambiguous lands on your plate?

---

## Section 4 — Fit

- Q16. What do you want from a high-signal systems circle?
- Q17. What do you not want?
- Q18. What would make this circle a waste of your time?

---

## Section 5 — Moral Orientation

- Q19. What governs your decisions when no rule requires it and no one is watching?
- Q20. What is your plumb line?

---

## Section 6 — System Representation

- Q21. Represent your system. Format is yours. Diagram, outline, schema, or prose. Hand-drawn and photographed is fine. Attach to your email.
